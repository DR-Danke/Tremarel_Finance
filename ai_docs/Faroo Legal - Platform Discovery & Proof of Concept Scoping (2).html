Executive Summary

Carlos, founder of Faroo Legal, presented his vision for a global legal services platform — an “Uber for Legal” — that connects clients with a curated ecosystem of 400–500 specialist lawyers across multiple geographies and legal domains. The team identified the core bottleneck as the manual assignment and operations workflow (currently run via WhatsApp, email, and Notion) and agreed to build a phased proof of concept starting with the specialist assignment engine and pricing engine. A follow-up meeting was scheduled for Friday, February 28, 2026 at 4:00 PM.

Key Discussion Points

1. Faroo Legal Business Model

Faroo Legal acts as a “Consigliere” — orchestrating the right specialists for each client need across countries and legal specialties. The platform addresses the fragmentation problem where clients must independently coordinate lawyers across jurisdictions.

2. Specialist Assignment & Routing Engine

The primary bottleneck is manual specialist assignment. An intelligent routing engine should process client requirements, match against the specialist database (skills, score, workload), and propose candidates for approval. Top priority for the POC.

3. Pricing Engine

A pricing motor determines specialist cost vs. client billing rate. After pre-selection, there is a negotiation phase, then Faroo adds its margin and presents a unified quote. Historical data should inform suggested price ranges.

4. Operations Pipeline & Case Management

Once a client accepts, operational tickets are created per case — tracking specialists, tasks, deliverables, deadlines, billing, and documents. Automated deadline alerts and overdue notifications are critical needs.

5. Commercial Funnel & CRM

Multi-channel leads flow through discovery calls to proposals. Go High Level recommended as CRM for the commercial funnel, with backend integrations to avoid dual-database management with Notion.

6. Specialist Database & Scoring

400–500 members including individuals and boutique firms, each with 8+ years of experience. A performance score based on quality, teamwork, delivery, and client satisfaction drives assignment priority.

7. Automatable Products vs. Complex Projects

Standardized products (e.g., trademark registration) should be nearly fully automated, while complex projects require deep specialist involvement. Small cases consume disproportionate management energy.
