## Discussion Points

### Budget/APU Manual Coding — Largest Pain Point
6-person team processes 10-12 budgets/week. Each budget requires up to 3 days of manual coding against an incomplete APU database in Excel. Items repeat across budgets but are re-coded manually. This blocks the team from performing technical plan reviews — a key competitive differentiator that won them major clients.

### Bank Reconciliation Process
5 bank accounts (2 savings, 1 checking, 2 trust/fiduciary) reconciled daily and monthly. One full-time person dedicated to daily egress reconciliation. ~30 accounting rules applied manually in Excel using VLOOKUP/CONCATENATE. Trust (fiducia) payments arrive without third-party detail, making matching extremely difficult.

### Purchase-to-Pay Chain (OC → RP → Invoice)
Critical gap in document support chain: suppliers don't always send supporting documents with invoices, job sites without warehouse staff can't certify receipt, and documents arrive across 16 fragmented channels (8 WhatsApp + 8 email accounts + invoicing email). Without complete supports, Accounting can't book entries — causing weekly report delays.

### Electronic Invoicing (RADIAN/DIAN)
500+ purchase invoices/month processed one-by-one for DIAN acceptance/rejection via XML. Previously required 2 full-time people. Recently discovered external provider for bulk XML download (~$13,000 COP for ~500 invoices), reducing workload to 2 hours.

### Inventory Management
47 warehouses (on-site and central), ~15,000 products. Manual Kardex in Excel backs up Word Office ERP. Warehouse staff report daily entries/exits via email (PDF). No historical analysis for procurement forecasting. Peak receiving: first 1.5 weeks of each month (~50 purchase orders/day).

### Illumination/Direct Sales Channel Issues
Sales team acts as both buyer and seller (conflict of interest), quoting suppliers and clients simultaneously. Prices fluctuate daily, making historical data useless. Sales commits delivery times without checking inventory or coordinating with Logistics.

### ERP Migration Strategy
Migrating from Word Office to Auranet (Chilean ERP specialized in construction), started November 2025. Dual-accounting during transition. Migration driven by Word Office's inability to provide per-project budget control. Currently in training phase.

### Executive Vision and Growth Plans
CEO does NOT want to reduce headcount — wants to free up capacity for new consortium contracts being signed. Areas still to be mapped: Engineering, OHS, HR, Public Projects. Requested a commercial proposal with automation phases.
