## Summary

Grupo G's directors met with the AI consulting team (Daniel & Daniel Restrepo) to evaluate their AI transformation proposal. The group expressed strong interest in Plan A (full process diagnosis at $6,000) to map all organizational processes and identify quick-win automation opportunities. Key concerns were raised around source code ownership, hosting model, NDA requirements, and database architecture strategy. ## Discussion Points

### Plan A vs Plan B Service Approach
Plan A is a comprehensive process diagnosis ($6,000) involving WhatsApp interviews with key staff to map all organizational processes, identify friction points, and find quick-win opportunities ('low-hanging fruit'). Plan B targets specific known pain points with custom AI solutions. The group favors Plan A but wants both to run in parallel.

### Invoicing Complexity as Primary Pain Point
Grupo G's invoicing process is highly complex and matrix-like: each client requires different billing for routes, warehousing, maquila (assembly), and transport. Multiple teams review and re-review before invoicing, leading to errors, client rejections, re-billing, and delayed collections. This was identified as the most critical operational bottleneck.

### Source Code Ownership & Hosting Model
Héctor raised concerns about who owns the developed tools. The consulting team explained the default model retains source code ownership with them, but offered to quote both options (client-owned vs consultant-owned code). Hosting can be on either party's infrastructure. The group wants both pricing structures in the formal proposal.

### Quotation System Demo (Tenderi Case Study)
A live demo was shown of a custom quotation system built for a Colombian logistics company. It reduced quotation time from 7-10 days to 15 minutes, with route configuration, regulatory integration for legal minimums, statistical market analysis, and QR-code-based client acceptance. Built in 24 days (vs 6-8 months traditional), costing $8,000-$10,000 one-time plus recurring usage fees.

### Bank Reconciliation Product
A pre-built bank reconciliation tool was demonstrated that matched ~90% of transactions in 5 minutes (vs 2-3 days manually). Priced at $4,000 one-time plus ~$150/month recurring. Less customization needed than quotation tools since back-office financial processes are more standardized.

### Database Architecture & Data Warehouse Strategy
Héctor requested that the proposal include recommendations for structuring the company's database to identify core revenue-generating processes and reduce administrative overhead. The consulting team described their approach: diagnose data flows between systems (e.g., SAP, Oracle), design a data warehouse architecture where systems feed data automatically without manual Excel manipulation.

### Revenue Analytics & Client Health Dashboard
A revenue intelligence dashboard was shown from a US freight forwarder engagement. It included client health scoring, quote-to-execution effectiveness rates, churn analysis, hidden gems identification, pipeline effectiveness by sales rep, payment risk assessment, and strategic revenue impact modeling (e.g., 5% churn reduction = $375K revenue).

### NDA & Confidentiality Requirements
Héctor emphasized the need for an NDA before sharing operational data, especially since the consulting team may work with other logistics companies. Daniel's team agreed to send the NDA.

### Existing PwC Process Map from 10 Years Ago
Héctor mentioned that PricewaterhouseCoopers created a process map for the company approximately 10 years ago (held by Pastor Oropesa). While useful as a baseline reference, all parties agreed it likely doesn't reflect current operations and should serve only as a guide, not a constraint for the new diagnosis.

### IT Capability Assessment
Grupo G's tech team is limited to basic IT support (infrastructure, networks) with no product development capability. This reinforces the outsourced development model, as even if source code were transferred, the internal team couldn't maintain it. The consulting team's vision is that future business teams will be empowered to use and enable technology without a separate IT department.

### Revenue Share Pricing Model
The consulting team mentioned a revenue share model used with another client, where they participate in revenue increases generated by their initiatives. This was presented as an alternative engagement model alongside fixed-fee development and recurring subscription fees.

## Decisions

- Diagnosis and proof-of-concept (POC) development will run in parallel rather than sequentially
- The proposal must include both pricing structures: client-owned source code vs consultant-owned source code
- An NDA must be signed before operational data is shared with the consulting team
- Database architecture recommendations will be included in the diagnosis scope
- The existing PwC process map will be used only as a reference guide, not as the authoritative baseline
