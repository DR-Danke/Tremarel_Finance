**Date:** 2026-03-03

## Summary

Grupo G, a Colombian logistics company (domestic transport, warehousing, and maquila services), evaluated an AI consulting proposal with two options: Plan A ($6K process mapping diagnostic) and Plan B (targeted solution development). The team demonstrated a quoting system (reduced 7-10 days to 15 minutes) and a bank reconciliation tool ($4K + $150/month). Key concerns raised were code ownership, hosting, NDA requirements, and database architecture. The group agreed to proceed with Plan A, potentially running a proof-of-concept in parallel, and requested an updated proposal with dual code-ownership pricing options.

## Discussion Points

### Billing & Invoicing Complexity
Grupo G faces severe billing complexity: multiple service types per client (transport, warehousing, maquila), non-standardized processes per client, frequent errors causing invoice rejections and delayed collections. This was identified as a major pain point consuming excessive team time.

### Plan A vs Plan B Proposal Evaluation
Plan A: Full organizational process mapping diagnostic ($6K) with WhatsApp interviews across key personnel, delivering flowcharts and opportunity assessment. Plan B: Targeted solution development for specific pain points ($8K-$10K for custom solutions). The group leans toward Plan A to identify the highest-impact opportunities ('low-hanging fruit').

### Code Ownership & IP Structure
Héctor raised critical concerns about who owns the source code and tools developed. Currently, the default model keeps source code with the development team. The prospect requested dual pricing: one where code stays with the vendor, another where Grupo G owns it outright. This must be clarified in the updated proposal.

### Quoting System Demo (Logistics Case Study)
Live demonstration of a custom quoting tool built for a Colombian logistics company. Features include Excel-based route configuration, regulatory integration for minimum legal pricing, statistical market analysis, QR-code enabled client interaction (accept/reject), and email delivery. Built in 24 days (vs. 6-8 months traditionally), priced at $8K-$10K. Reduced quoting time from 7-10 days to 15 minutes.

### Bank Reconciliation Tool
A semi-standardized product offered at $4K setup + $150/month recurring. Achieves ~90% automatic matching in 5 minutes vs. 2-3 days of manual work. Less customization needed compared to quoting solutions. Already supports Colombian bank formats.

### Database Architecture & Data Warehouse Strategy
Héctor requested that the proposal include recommendations for structuring the company's database to identify core revenue-generating processes and reduce administrative overhead. Referenced a Bolivia multi-family office case where SAP Business One and Oracle data were consolidated into a data warehouse, eliminating $500K/year in reprocessing costs.

### Revenue Analytics & Client Health Monitoring
Demonstration of a 3-4 month engagement with a US freight forwarder showing client health scoring: quote effectiveness rates, revenue trends by rep/department, churn analysis, hidden gem identification, payment risk signals, and strategic action plans. Showed how data-driven decisions can recover $375K+ from churn reduction alone.

### NDA & Confidentiality Requirements
Héctor emphasized the need for an NDA to protect Grupo G's proprietary information, especially as the AI services team may work with competing logistics companies in the future.

### Hosting & Ongoing Relationship Model
Solutions are cloud-hosted (can be vendor's or client's domain). Three cost components: implementation/development, subscription/maintenance, and per-use fees. Grupo G wants clarity on long-term contractual commitments to avoid heavy structural costs.

### Existing PwC Process Map
PricewaterhouseCoopers created a comprehensive process map for the company ~10 years ago. It will serve as a baseline reference, but both parties agreed that current processes have likely diverged significantly and the diagnostic should capture the as-is state, not anchor to outdated documentation.

### Technology Team Assessment
Grupo G's internal tech team is limited to IT support (infrastructure, networks) with no product development capability. This supports the outsourced development model and reinforces that code ownership is more symbolic than practical for the client.
