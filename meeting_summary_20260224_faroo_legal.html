Executive Summary

Discovery call with Carlos, co-founder of Farú Legal, a global legal services platform that operates as a "legal cloud" — connecting enterprise clients with a curated ecosystem of 400–500 specialist lawyers across multiple jurisdictions and practice areas. The meeting mapped the full operational flow from lead intake to case delivery and billing. The team agreed to build a proof of concept starting with 1–2 case types (e.g., trademark registration) and 3–4 specialists. A follow-up meeting is scheduled for Friday, February 28 at 4:00 PM.

Key Discussion Points

1. Platform Architecture & Operational Flow

End-to-end flow mapped: leads enter through multiple channels → discovery call → requirements extraction → specialist assignment → pricing negotiation → client quote → operational tickets → case management with task tracking, deliverables, and billing. Three data layers identified: commercial, operations, and specialist databases.

2. Specialist Assignment Engine (Core Need)

Primary bottleneck is manual specialist assignment. Carlos has an undocumented heuristic for matching cases to specialists based on specialty, jurisdiction, performance score, workload, and teamwork ability. This must be documented and automated — potentially with AI for complex cases.

3. Pricing Engine

System should suggest price ranges based on historical specialist rates, allow Farú Legal to add margin, and generate a unified client quote. Payment model: specialists are paid within 1–2 days of receiving client payment.

4. Specialist Ecosystem

400–500 lawyers including individuals and boutique/mid-size firms. Minimum 8 years experience. Performance scoring based on quality, timeliness, and collaboration. Dual intake: cases can come from clients directly or via specialist referrals.

5. Current Tools & Pain Points

Operations managed manually in Notion (cases, tasks, billing, Google Drive). Go High Level CRM started but abandoned to avoid dual databases. All coordination via WhatsApp and email. Recommendation: keep Go High Level for commercial funnel only.

6. Two Operational Models

Standardized products (e.g., trademark registration) should be fully automated to free up capacity. Complex cross-border projects require strategic Concilieri involvement. Both must be served — small cases are a gateway to major client relationships.

7. AI & Agent Opportunities

Carlos envisions a hybrid community of human lawyers and AI agents. Potential AI use: processing call transcripts to extract requirements, automatic specialist matching, automated alerts, and end-to-end automation of standardized products.

Decisions Made

  • Proceed with a phased proof-of-concept approach rather than building the full system at once
  • POC scope: specialist assignment engine with 1–2 case types and 3–4 specialists
  • Drop Go High Level as standalone CRM; consider keeping it for the commercial funnel only
  • Platform designed as a service desk with embedded AI for assignment and classification
