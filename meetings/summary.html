Executive Summary

Discovery meeting to explore automation opportunities in Lutec's construction budgeting process. Lutec is an electrical construction subcontractor with a 6-person team producing 10–12 budgets per week. The main bottleneck is manually matching client-provided Excel activity lists against their internal APU (Unit Price Analysis) database of materials, labor, and suppliers. The proposed automation would use similarity matching to automatically suggest the closest APU codes for each client activity, freeing the team to focus on higher-value work like BIM analysis, anomaly detection, and cost projection.

Discussion Points

1. Core Pain Point: Manual APU Matching Process
The budgeting team spends excessive time manually searching their internal APU (Unit Price Analysis) database to match client-requested construction activities. Each activity must be coded with the correct APU containing materials, labor, and supplier information. This manual process can take up to 3 days per budget and is highly repetitive.
2. Client Excel Format Constraints
Each construction client sends their own custom Excel spreadsheet with a quantity sheet. Lutec cannot modify the client's file format. Different clients have different structures and levels of specification detail, adding complexity to the matching process.
3. APU Database Structure and Categorization
Lutec maintains an internal database of APUs organized by product families (tubing, cables, boxes, plates, etc.) with sub-categories for type, installation method, diameter, and number of ducts. Each APU contains required materials with quantities, labor costs, waste percentages, and supplier mappings.
4. Proposed Automation: Similarity-Based Code Matching
The proposed solution would automatically analyze client Excel items and match them against Lutec's APU database using similarity analysis. The system would suggest the closest matching APU for each activity, and Oscar's team would only need to review, validate, and handle exceptions for unmatched items.
5. BIM Integration for Specification Verification
Lutec has a dedicated BIM specialist who reviews 3D construction models to verify specifications, quantities, and identify anomalies (e.g., unknown installation heights for luminaires). BIM analysis is critical for accurate costing but is deprioritized due to time spent on manual Excel work.
6. End-to-End Budget Workflow
The full workflow spans: receiving client Excel, matching activities to APU codes, associating materials/labor, identifying missing products, sourcing suppliers, passing info to Purchasing for negotiation, applying margin/profitability calculations, and returning the completed quotation in the client's original Excel format.
7. Post-Quotation Cost Control
After quotation approval, Lutec generates a consolidated supply list for the project. This becomes the cost control baseline, where deviations impact margin and profitability.
8. Team Size and Volume
The budgeting department has 6 people working full-time, producing 10–12 budgets per week. One team member is dedicated exclusively to BIM analysis.
