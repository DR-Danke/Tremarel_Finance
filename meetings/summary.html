Key Discussion Points
1. Budget / APU Manual Coding — Largest Pain Point
6-person team processes 10-12 budgets/week. Each budget requires up to 3 days of manual coding against an incomplete APU database in Excel. Items repeat across budgets but are re-coded manually. This blocks the team from performing technical plan reviews — a key competitive differentiator that won them major clients.
2. Bank Reconciliation Process
5 bank accounts reconciled daily and monthly. One full-time person dedicated to daily egress reconciliation. ~30 accounting rules applied manually in Excel. Trust (fiducia) payments arrive without payer detail, making matching extremely difficult.
3. Purchase-to-Pay Chain (OC → RP → Invoice)
Critical gap: suppliers don't always send supporting documents, job sites without warehouse staff can't certify receipt, documents arrive across 16 fragmented channels. Without complete supports, Accounting can't book entries — causing weekly report delays.
4. Electronic Invoicing (RADIAN/DIAN)
500+ purchase invoices/month processed one-by-one for DIAN acceptance/rejection via XML. Previously required 2 full-time people. Recently discovered external provider for bulk XML download, reducing workload to 2 hours.
5. Inventory Management
47 warehouses, ~15,000 products. Manual Kardex in Excel backs up Word Office. No historical analysis for procurement forecasting. Peak receiving: first 1.5 weeks of each month (~50 OC/day).
6. Illumination / Direct Sales Channel Issues
Sales team acts as both buyer and seller (conflict of interest). Prices fluctuate daily making historical data useless. Sales commits delivery times without checking inventory or coordinating with Logistics.
7. ERP Migration Strategy
Migrating from Word Office to Auranet (Chilean ERP, construction-specialized), started November 2025. Dual-accounting during transition. Currently in training phase.
8. Executive Vision & Growth Plans
CEO wants to free capacity for new consortium contracts, not reduce headcount. Areas still to map: Engineering, OHS, HR, Public Projects.
