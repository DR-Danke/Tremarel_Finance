Executive Summary
Grupo G, a Colombian logistics company operating in domestic transport, warehousing, and maquila services, evaluated an AI consulting proposal with two options: Plan A ($6K process mapping diagnostic) and Plan B (targeted solution development, $8K-$10K per solution). The team demonstrated a quoting system that reduced turnaround from 7-10 days to 15 minutes, and a bank reconciliation tool achieving ~90% auto-matching. Key concerns raised include code ownership, hosting structure, NDA requirements, and database architecture. The group agreed to proceed with Plan A, potentially running a proof-of-concept in parallel, and requested an updated proposal with dual code-ownership pricing options.

Discussion Points
1. Billing & Invoicing Complexity
Grupo G faces severe billing complexity: multiple service types per client (transport, warehousing, maquila), non-standardized processes per client, frequent errors causing invoice rejections and delayed collections. This was identified as the major operational pain point consuming excessive team time and directly impacting cash flow.
2. Plan A vs Plan B Proposal Evaluation
Plan A: Full organizational process mapping diagnostic ($6K) with WhatsApp interviews across key personnel, delivering flowcharts and opportunity assessment. Plan B: Targeted solution development for specific pain points ($8K-$10K for custom solutions). The group prefers Plan A to identify the highest-impact opportunities ("low-hanging fruit") before committing to specific developments.
3. Code Ownership & Intellectual Property
Hector raised critical concerns about source code ownership and IP. Currently, the default model keeps source code with the development team. The prospect requested dual pricing: one where code stays with the vendor, another where Grupo G owns it outright. Must be clarified in the updated proposal. Practical concern: Grupo G's IT team lacks development capacity to maintain code even if owned.
4. Quoting System Demo (Case Study)
Live demonstration of a custom quoting tool for a Colombian logistics company. Features: Excel route upload, regulatory API integration for minimum legal pricing, statistical market analysis, QR-code client interaction, email delivery. Built in 24 days vs. 6-8 months traditionally. Reduced quoting time from 7-10 days to 15 minutes.
5. Bank Reconciliation Tool
Semi-standardized product at $4K setup + $150/month recurring. Achieves ~90% automatic matching in 5 minutes vs. 2-3 days of manual work. Less customization needed compared to quoting solutions. Already supports Colombian bank formats with adaptability for others.
6. Database Architecture & Data Warehouse
Hector requested the proposal include database structuring recommendations to identify core revenue-generating processes. Referenced a Bolivia case study where SAP Business One and Oracle data were consolidated into a data warehouse, eliminating $500K/year in reprocessing costs across 8 people manually crossing Excel files.
7. Revenue Analytics & Client Health
Demonstration of a 3-4 month engagement with a US freight forwarder: client health scoring, quote effectiveness rates, revenue trends by rep/department, churn analysis, hidden gem identification, payment risk signals, and strategic action plans. Data showed $375K recovery potential from 5% churn reduction alone.
8. NDA & Confidentiality
Hector emphasized the need for an NDA to protect proprietary information, especially as the AI services team may work with competing logistics companies.
9. Hosting & Ongoing Relationship
Cloud-hosted solutions with flexibility on domain ownership. Three cost components: implementation, subscription/maintenance, and per-use fees. Grupo G wants clarity on long-term contractual structure to avoid heavy overhead.
10. Existing PwC Process Map
PricewaterhouseCoopers created a comprehensive process map ~10 years ago (held by Pastor). Will serve as baseline reference, but both parties agreed current processes have likely diverged significantly. Diagnostic should capture the as-is state independently.
